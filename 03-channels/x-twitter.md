# 𝕏 X / Twitter Playbook

## Why X First

- Best distribution for B2B founder content
- Gym owners and fitness entrepreneurs are here
- Threads get organic reach that no other B2B channel matches
- Fastest feedback loop on messaging
- Builds Kevin's personal brand + Fiitsio brand simultaneously

---

## Account Strategy

### Kevin's Personal Account (@kvn_eth or similar)
**Voice:** Founder building in public, gym business insights, Portuguese market perspective

**Ratio:** 60% education / 20% company updates / 20% personality

### @Fiitsio
**Voice:** The gym OS brand — bolder, more opinionated
**Ratio:** 40% education / 30% competitor angles / 30% product proof

| Day | Content Type | Example |
|-----|-------------|---------|
| Monday | Insight thread | "5 things gym owners get wrong about pricing" |

**Goal:** 2 threads + 10+ tweets/week by month 3

## Thread Formula That Works

### Structure:
```
[Hook tweet — bold claim or curiosity gap]

[Tweet 2] Context / Why this matters

[Tweet 3-6] The meat — specific, numbered, actionable

[Tweet 7] The twist or surprising insight

[Tweet 8] The CTA — soft sell to Fiitsio

"If you want [outcome], this is why we built Fiitsio."
[link]
```

### Example Thread:

```
Tweet 1:
Most gyms lose 25% of their members every 3 months.
They don't know it's happening until it's too late.

Here's how to predict churn before it happens 🧵

Tweet 2:
First, let's talk about why members leave.
It's never "I found a better gym."
It's always "I stopped feeling like it mattered."

Tweet 3:
Sign #1: Drop in booking frequency.
If someone goes from 4x/week to 2x/week for 14 days —
that's not a holiday. That's the beginning of the end.

[continues...]

Tweet 8:
We built Fiitsio specifically to catch these signals automatically.
You shouldn't need to manually track 200 members in a spreadsheet.

If this resonates → fiitsio.com
```

## Engagement Rules

1. **Reply to everyone** in the first hour — algorithm rewards it
2. **Comment on gym owner accounts** daily — 5 meaningful comments
3. **Quote tweet** competitors' bad takes — never attack, just offer perspective
4. **Pin your best thread** always
5. **No posting and ghosting** — engagement drives reach

---

## Growth Tactics

### "Reply guy" strategy
Find the top 10 gym owner accounts on X. Reply to their posts daily with insightful comments. Don't promote Fiitsio. Build relationship first.

**Target accounts:** (research and list the top gym owner / fitness entrepreneur accounts)

### Thread recycling
Your best threads from 6+ months ago: repost them. New followers haven't seen them. Same thread, same content, still valuable.

### Controversy as content
When Mindbody announces a price increase → post about it.
When a gym software competitor has an outage → post about it.
When industry news drops → be first with the gym owner perspective.
